Many homes in Saskatoon were built with 100-amp electrical panels — and for the appliances of the time, that was plenty. But modern homes have very different demands: EV chargers, high-efficiency HVAC, large kitchen appliances, home offices. Here's how to tell if your panel needs an upgrade.

## Signs You May Need a Panel Upgrade

**Breakers that trip frequently** — If breakers are tripping more than occasionally, it's a sign your panel is struggling to keep up with demand.

**Flickering or dimming lights** — Lights that flicker when appliances start up indicate your circuits may be overloaded.

**Burning smell or warm panel** — Any smell of burning near your electrical panel is a serious warning sign. Turn off the main breaker and call a licensed electrician in Saskatoon immediately.

**You want to add a hot tub, EV charger, or major appliance** — These high-draw devices typically require a dedicated circuit, and adding them to an undersized panel isn't safe.

**You still have a fuse box** — Fuse boxes are outdated and most insurance companies in Saskatchewan won't cover a home with one. An upgrade to a modern breaker panel is essential.

## What a 200-Amp Upgrade Involves

A 200-amp panel upgrade involves replacing the service entrance cable (the wire coming in from the street), the meter base, and the breaker panel. In most cases, the job takes one day. Power will be off to your home for several hours while the work is done.

Your licensed electrician will pull the required permit from the City of Saskatoon or SaskPower and arrange for a final inspection to ensure the work meets code.

## Cost

Panel upgrades in Saskatoon typically range from $2,000–$4,500 depending on the scope of work and whether any additional wiring needs to be run. Getting a proper estimate from a licensed electrician is the only way to know for sure.
